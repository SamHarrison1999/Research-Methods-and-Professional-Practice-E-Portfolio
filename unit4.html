Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

← Previous Unit Next Unit →

Unit Overview

In Unit 4, we explored key qualitative data collection methods, including case studies, focus groups, and observations.

Learning Objectives

  • Learn how to conduct case studies, focus groups, and observational research.
  • Identify the appropriate method for your investigation based on its goals and requirements.
  • Understand the types of data obtained from each method and how they support research goals.

This section includes the artefacts completed during this unit.

Literature Review Outline

Research Topic: AI-based assistive technology for the physically disabled and/or the elderly

What is the focus and aim of your review? Who is your audience?

  • How can assistive technology improve a disabled person's quality of life?
  • What are the benefits of AI-based assistive technology?
  • What are the ethical, legal and social concerns relevant to assistive technology, more specifically AI based assistive technology?
  • What are the drawbacks to assistive technology? (cost, negative perception etc.)
  • The target audience is the disabled, the elderly and their carers
    • The goal is to educate them on the assistive technologies available to them

Why is there a need for your review? Why is it significant?

  • Because some people with disabilities may not be aware of the assistive technologies available to them
    • Possible reasons for this; they were diagnosed later in life so may not have been aware of their disabilities, they were unaware of the assistive technologies available to them, they may have severe mental deficiencies so they may be unable to understand the information that has been provided to them on assistive technologies in the past
  • Assistive technologies could help to safeguard the vulnerable
  • Assistive technologies could improve the quality of life for people with severe disabilities
  • Assistive technologies can help people who struggle with daily living activities and may allow them to become more independent

What is the context of the topic or issue? What perspective do you take? What framework do you use to synthesise the literature?

  • The context of the topic is going to be centred around how assistive technologies can improve vulnerable people’s quality of life allowing them to be more independent
  • While I will mainly be looking at the perspective of how assistive technology has improved their life, I will also touch on the drawbacks of assistive technology such as the overreliance on it.
  • I will be using Chris Hart’s book on literature reviews (Hart, 2018) in conjunction with Harris Cooper’s article on literature reviews (Cooper, 1988) for guidance when writing the literature review

Access to Artificial Intelligence for Persons with Disabilities: Legal and Ethical Questions Concerning the Application of Trustworthy AI

  • The article by Dr Kristi Joamets and Dr Archil Chochia looks at the legal, social and ethical issues of ai-based assistive technologies for those with disabilities
  • They argue that a national policy to enforce the rights of disabled people is needed
  • As a vulnerable adult myself, I found the article to be rather informative
  • Strengths:
    • Highlights the ability of assistive technologies to improve a disabled person's quality of life
    • Highlights how Artificial Intelligence can enhance assistive technologies
    • Emphasises the importance of trustworthy AI referencing relevant EU legislation and ethical guidelines
    • Provided a solid background on the laws surrounding Artificial Intelligence and the rights of people with disabilities
  • Weaknesses:
    • The article heavily focused on European legal and ethical standards. The article could have benefited from providing more of a global perspective on the relevant legal, social and ethical legislation for AI-based assistive technologies.
      • What about the relevant legislation in regions other than Europe?
    • The article could have gone into more detail on the benefits, risks and limitations of AI as they only looked at them at a high level.
  • Further Research:
    • How different cultural and legal contexts affect the adoption and ethics of AI
    • How AI can aid those with specific disabilities such as those with dementia

Economic evaluation of healthcare technology improving the quality of social life: the case of assistive technology for the disabled and elderly’

  • The article by Jungwoo Shin, Yeunjoong Kim, Heekoo Nam & Youngsang Cho analyses the socio-economic value of assistive technologies using the Spike model
  • Strengths:
    • Used the spike model to handle the scenario when respondents indicated zero willingness to pay for government investments in assistive technologies
    • Used contingent valuation method (a widely accepted method)
    • Provided quantitative insights
      • Social economic benefit
      • WTP
    • Identified key social demographic factors that influence WTP
    • Identified attitudes towards assistive technologies
    • Provided a recommendation on how to decrease the number of respondents who indicated zero WTP
  • Weaknesses:
    • The study focused on respondents from Seoul and Gyeonggi Province. Other areas may have very different perspectives
    • Results rely on a hypothetical willingness so may not be accurate
    • Only focused on five different types of assistive technologies
    • No longitudinal analysis
    • Didn’t investigate the indirect benefits of assistive technologies
  • Further Research:
    • Include other regions in South Korea in research
    • Compare the hypothetical WTP with the actual payments
    • Do a longitudinal study to show how attitudes and WTP have changed over time
    • Research the indirect benefits
    • Gather qualitative data

Case Study on Privacy

If Ricardo is not responsible for determining allowable access, should he release the names and addresses?

Ricardo should not release the information because it could be considered a breach of confidentiality, because he doesn’t have permission to release the information and because it could cause legal issues.

Suppose Ricardo were responsible for determining allowable access to the files. What ethical issues would be involved in his deciding whether to grant access to Beth?

The ethical issues Ricardo would need to consider are related to an individual’s right to privacy against the potential scientific benefit. He would have to make sure that he has obtained informed consent, is not abusing his position, hasn’t set any precedent and isn't breaking any legal or institutional policies.

Should Beth be allowed to contact the individuals involved? That is, should the Records department release individuals' names to a researcher? What are the ethical issues for the Records department to consider?

The ethical issues the Records department must consider are related to the individual’s right to privacy, consent, public trust, confidentiality and potential harm before deciding whether Beth should be allowed to contact the individuals involved in the study.

Suppose Beth contacts the individuals to ask their permission, and one-third of them respond giving permission, one-third respond denying permission, and one-third do not respond. Beth claims that at least one-half of the individuals are necessary to make a valid study. What options are available to Beth?

There are several options available to Beth; she could limit her study to the individuals who gave permission, she could consult an ethical review board for guidance, she could reattempt to contact those who did not respond, or she could modify her research design.
