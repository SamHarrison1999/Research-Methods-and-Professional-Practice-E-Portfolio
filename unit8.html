Unit 8: Data Analysis and Visualisation

← Previous Unit Next Unit →

Unit Overview

In Unit 8, you will explore techniques for analysing, interpreting, and presenting both qualitative and quantitative data, focusing on visualising data effectively through charts and dashboards to communicate meaningful insights.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand and apply methods for analysing qualitative and quantitative data
  • Learn how to present data effectively using appropriate charts and visual tools.
  • Recognize the components and functionality of dashboards for business intelligence
  • Develop critical thinking skills in evaluating and interpreting graphical representations of data.

Artefacts

Inference Worksheet — Portfolio Activity
How the artefacts relate to the module objectives

The Inference Worksheet in Unit 8 is directly aligned with the module objectives, as it strengthens students' ability to apply inferential statistical methods to computing research, a fundamental skill in academic and professional practice. By working through the worksheet, students gained hands-on experience in analysing data, drawing meaningful conclusions from statistical tests, and interpreting results within the context of research inquiries.

One of the key module objectives is to appraise the principles of academic investigation and apply them to a research topic in the applicable computing field. The worksheet required students to work with quantitative data, reinforcing their ability to choose appropriate statistical methods, conduct hypothesis testing, and validate research findings. These skills are particularly relevant in fields such as data science, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and software engineering, where data-driven decision-making is essential.

Additionally, the worksheet supports the objective of evaluating critically existing literature, research design, and methodology, including data analysis processes. Inferential statistics play a crucial role in research validity, as they allow researchers to generalise findings from a sample to a larger population. Through this exercise, students learned how to apply statistical techniques rigorously, ensuring that their research is methodologically sound and supported by valid data analysis.

Furthermore, the Inference Worksheet relates to the module objective of appraising the professional, legal, social, cultural, and ethical issues that affect computing professionals. The misuse of statistical methods—whether intentional or unintentional—can lead to misleading conclusions, biased research outcomes, and ethical concerns in computing. By completing this activity, students gained a deeper understanding of the importance of ethical data analysis, ensuring that statistical methods are applied transparently, accurately, and responsibly in computing research.

Finally, this worksheet contributes to the objective of producing and critically evaluating a research proposal. A research proposal must include a clear plan for data collection, statistical analysis, and interpretation of results. By engaging in inferential statistics exercises, students improved their ability to justify their chosen research methods and interpret data effectively, which is essential when preparing a well-structured research proposal.

In conclusion, the Inference Worksheet plays a crucial role in achieving the module’s objectives by enhancing students' understanding of inferential statistics, strengthening their data analysis skills, reinforcing ethical research practices, and improving their ability to design robust research proposals. This activity provided a practical application of statistical concepts, ensuring that students are equipped with the necessary tools to conduct rigorous and ethical computing research.

Research Proposal Outline

Project Title

The title of the research proposal is Harnessing Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Assistive Technologies for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD): Bridging Gaps in Independence, Education, Accessibility, Ethics and Employment.

Introduction

  • Overview of ASD
  • How assistive technologies are used to address the challenges faced by individuals with ASD
  • How AI is being integrated into assistive technologies

Significance/Contribution to the discipline/Research Problem

ASD affects approximately 1 in 100 children globally (World Health Organization, 2023). Individuals with autism typically struggle with communication, social interaction and independence. The integration of AI into assistive technologies creates new opportunities for personalised solutions. For example, AI tools such as adaptive learning platforms, wearable devices and job matching systems have the potential to enhance independence, confidence and employability.

There are significant gaps in the current literature in understanding the long-term effectiveness, ethical implications and accessibility of these technologies. This research proposal aims to fill these gaps by focusing on the need for inclusive and sustainable solutions that improve the quality of life for individuals with ASD.

Research Question

The central question this research aims to answer is how can AI-based assistive technologies be optimised to enhance independence, confidence, education, safety and employability for individuals with ASD while addressing ethical and accessibility challenges.

Aims and Objectives

The primary aim is to critically evaluate the potential and limitations of AI-based assistive technologies for individuals with ASD.

Research objectives

  1. Evaluate existing AI-based assistive tools
  2. Evaluate the efficacy of AI-based assistive tools
  3. Explore the cultural and demographic adaptability of AI-based assistive tools Evaluate accessibility barriers of AI-based assistive tools
  4. Identify gaps and limitations in the current solutions
  5. Highlight the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration for developing AI-based assistive tools

Key literature related to the project

  • Emotion recognition systems have shown promise in improving social skills and emotional intelligence of individuals with ASD (Sahin et al., 2021)
  • Social robots provide safe non-judgemental environments for practicing social interactions and have been shown to help reduce anxiety and improve self-confidence (Pennisi et al., 2016)
  • Adaptive learning platforms such as BrainPro provide personalised content and have shown to improve academic engagement (Zhou et al., 2020)
  • Adaptive learning platforms have demonstrated a 40% improvement in academic outcomes compared to traditional methods (Kientz, Hayes, and Abowd, 2020)
  • Accessibility is limited due to high costs and infrastructure requirements (Parsons et al., 2019)
  • Privacy concerns related to ethical compliance and informed consent remain huge obstacles that are yet to be overcome (Rozenblum et al., 2021)
  • Studies on the impact of AI on independence, education, and employment outcomes (Kim et al., 2021; Parsons et al., 2018)
  • Ethical concerns in AI (Floridi et al., 2018; Shen et al., 2020)
  • Research on cultural adaptability and accessibility challenges (Chunara et al., 2021; Schmidt et al., 2019)
  • Long-term impacts
  • Potential risks of over reliance and suppressed natural coping mechanisms (Lindsay et al., 2020)
  • Effectiveness of wearable AI tools in reducing wandering incidents (Goodwin et al., 2020).
  • Discussion of increased task completion rates and emotional empowerment (Kim et al., 2021).

Methodology/Development strategy/Research Design

  • Systematic literature review to select sources then a thematic synthesis to categorise findings into key areas then a comparative analysis of technologies based on key metrics
  • Using solely secondary data due to time constraints
  • Using a combination of quantitative and qualitative data to provide a balanced perspective on technical efficacy and real-world applicability
  • Comparative evaluation of technologies

Ethical considerations and risk assessment (as part of your ethical approval application)

  • Privacy of sensitive data collected (Shen, Hong, and Smith, 2020)
  • Data breaches or misuse could harm vulnerable populations (Barocas et al., 2019)
  • Informed consent as users or caregivers may not understand how their data is processed or used (Crawford and Calo, 2016)
  • Algorithmic bias (Suresh and Guttag, 2021)
  • Selection bias
  • Ensuring data privacy and informed consent in all referenced sources

Description of artefact(s) that will be created (if applicable)

No physical artefacts will be created as the research focuses on a critical evaluation of existing literature.

Timeline of proposed activities

The research will be completed over 8 weeks. During the first two weeks, I will focus solely on searching for and selecting the relevant sources. Over the next two weeks, I will focus on the thematic synthesis and begin drafting up the key sections for independence, education, employability and safety. Over the next two weeks, I will dive into the long-term impacts, ethical challenges and cultural challenges. In the penultimate week, I will focus on the strengths, limitations and recommendations. Then in the final week I will be finishing off the research proposal.

Conclusion

While AI-based assistive technologies have a transformative potential to positively impact the lives of individuals with ASD, there are plenty of challenges that they are yet to overcome. Future research should focus on longitudinal studies to demonstrate the long-term impacts of AI-based assistive technologies.

How the artefact relates to the module objectives

The Unit 8 Reflection is directly aligned with the module objectives, as it enables students to critically assess their learning experiences related to data analysis and visualisation. This reflection encourages the development of self-awareness, critical thinking, and evaluation skills, all of which are integral to the study of research methods and professional practice in computing.

One key module objective that the reflection supports is the ability to evaluate critically existing literature, research design, and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes. In Unit 8, students engage with different qualitative and quantitative data analysis techniques, visualisation tools, and business intelligence methodologies. By reflecting on their experience with these methods, students assess their own competency, highlight challenges they encountered, and identify areas for improvement in interpreting and presenting data effectively. This process ensures that they develop a deeper understanding of data analysis approaches and their application in computing research.

The reflection also ties into the objective of appraising the principles of academic investigation and applying them to a research topic in computing. Through the self-assessment of data interpretation and visualisation skills, students consider how well they have applied statistical and analytical methods to real-world computing problems. This includes evaluating how effectively they have used tools such as Power BI for data analysis, which is essential for conducting research in computing fields like artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and big data analytics.

Additionally, the reflection aligns with the objective of appraising the professional, legal, social, cultural, and ethical issues that affect computing professionals. Data analysis is inherently tied to ethical considerations, particularly when dealing with data privacy, bias in machine learning models, and data-driven decision-making. By reflecting on their experiences with data visualisation and interpretation, students can assess whether they have accounted for ethical considerations, such as data integrity, transparency, and avoiding misleading visual representations.

Another objective that the Unit 8 Reflection supports is producing and critically evaluating the research proposal for the chosen topic. Data analysis and visualisation are crucial components of any research project, as they help transform raw data into meaningful insights. Reflecting on the effectiveness of different analysis methods allows students to refine their approach to presenting findings in their research proposal, ensuring that their data supports their hypothesis and research objectives.

In conclusion, the Unit 8 Reflection plays a significant role in meeting the module objectives by enabling students to critically evaluate their progress in data analysis and visualisation, consider the ethical implications of data interpretation, and refine their approach to academic research in computing. Through this reflection, students develop a greater awareness of their strengths and areas for improvement, ensuring that they can effectively apply data analysis techniques in future research and professional contexts.
