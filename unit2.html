Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

← Previous Unit Next Unit →

Unit Overview

In Unit 2, we learnt how to define and refine research questions, craft a research proposal, and conduct a literature review.

Learning Objectives

  • Formulate and refine research questions effectively.
  • Understand the components of a research proposal and present ideas clearly.
  • Explain the purpose and process of conducting a literature review.
  • Identify characteristics of a suitable research topic.
  • Apply both rational and creative methods to develop research ideas.
  • Transform research ideas into well-defined research questions and proposals.
  • Conduct a comprehensive literature search, critically evaluate the findings, and present a literature review.

How the artefact relates to the module objectives

The Literature Review Plan is closely related to the module objectives, as it plays a crucial role in the research process by ensuring a structured and critical approach to reviewing existing academic work. This artefact directly supports the goal of appraising professional, legal, social, cultural, and ethical issues affecting computing professionals, as conducting a literature review requires an awareness of ethical considerations, including proper citation, avoiding plagiarism, and evaluating sources for credibility. By planning the literature review, students ensure that they critically assess research studies that address key ethical challenges in computing, such as data privacy, bias in artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity concerns.

Furthermore, the Literature Review Plan aligns with the objective of appraising the principles of academic investigation, as it requires students to structure their research in a way that supports systematic inquiry. Academic research is built upon previous findings, and a well-developed literature review ensures that students engage with relevant theories, frameworks, and methodologies. By planning how to collect, evaluate, and synthesise sources, students learn to apply academic research principles effectively, ensuring their work is well-grounded in existing knowledge.

This artefact also supports the critical evaluation of existing literature, research design, and methodology. The literature review process involves analysing past studies to identify research gaps, assess the strengths and weaknesses of different methodologies, and determine the most relevant approaches for a given topic. By planning their literature review, students develop skills in comparing and contrasting different studies, evaluating the reliability of research findings, and selecting the most appropriate frameworks for their own investigation. This critical evaluation is essential for developing a strong research foundation and making informed methodological choices.

Additionally, the Literature Review Plan is integral to producing and critically evaluating a research proposal. A well-structured literature review provides the theoretical background for a research proposal, justifying the significance of the research problem and establishing the rationale for the chosen methodology. By outlining how key studies relate to their research question, students ensure that their proposal is grounded in existing knowledge and that their investigation will contribute meaningfully to the field. The ability to review and critique literature is crucial in demonstrating academic rigour and ensuring that research proposals are logically structured and methodologically sound.

Overall, the Literature Review Plan is a foundational artefact that helps students develop key research competencies while aligning with the module objectives. It reinforces ethical research practices, promotes critical engagement with academic literature, and supports the development of a robust research proposal by ensuring that students have a comprehensive understanding of their chosen topic.
