Unit 2: Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

← Previous Unit Next Unit →

Unit Overview

In Unit 2, we learnt how to define and refine research questions, craft a research proposal, and conduct a literature review.

Learning Objectives

  • Formulate and refine research questions effectively.
  • Understand the components of a research proposal and present ideas clearly.
  • Explain the purpose and process of conducting a literature review.
  • Identify characteristics of a suitable research topic.
  • Apply both rational and creative methods to develop research ideas.
  • Transform research ideas into well-defined research questions and proposals.
  • Conduct a comprehensive literature search, critically evaluate the findings, and present a literature review.

This section includes the artefacts completed during this unit.

Research Topic: AI-based assistive technology for the physically disabled and/or the elderly

What is the focus and aim of your review? Who is your audience?

  • How can assistive technology improve a disabled persons quality of life?
  • What are the benefits of AI based assistive technology?
  • What are the ethical, legal and social concerns relevant to assistive technology, more specifically AI based assistive technology?
  • What are the drawbacks to assistive technology? (cost, negative perception etc.)
  • The target audience is the disabled, the elderly and their carers
    • The goal is to educate them on the assistive technologies available to them

Why is there a need for your review? Why is it significant?

  • Because some people with disabilities may not be aware of the assistive technologies available to them
    • Possible reasons for this; they were diagnosed later in life so may not have been aware of their disabilities, they are unaware of the assistive technologies available to them, they may have severe mental deficiencies so may be unable to understand the information that has been provided to them on assistive technologies in the past
  • Assistive technologies could help to safeguard the vulnerable
  • Assistive technologies could improve the quality of life for people with severe disabilities
  • Assistive technologies can help people who struggle with daily living activities and may allow them to become more independent

What is the context of the topic or issue? What perspective do you take? What framework do you want to use to synthesise the literature?

  • The context of the topic is going to be centred around how assistive technologies can improve vulnerable people’s quality of life allowing them to be more independent
  • While I will mainly be looking at the perspective of how assistive technology has improved their life, I will also touch on the drawbacks of assistive technology such as the over reliance on it.
  • I will be using Chris Hart’s book on literature reviews (Hart, 2018) in conjunction with Harris Cooper’s article on literature reviews (Cooper, 1988) for guidance when writing the literature review

Reflection

Description

Unit 2 of the Research Methods and Professional Practice module focused on the principles of academic investigation, particularly within the field of computing research. This unit emphasized the importance of defining research problems, formulating research questions, and understanding different research paradigms. Additionally, it introduced key aspects of literature reviews and source evaluation, which are essential for conducting credible academic research.

One of the most significant components of this unit was engaging with various academic sources and critically assessing their relevance to research inquiries. The activities involved identifying gaps in existing research, assessing the credibility of sources, and learning how to structure a literature review effectively. Furthermore, the unit explored the ethical and professional considerations in computing research, reinforcing the necessity of integrity and transparency in academic investigations.

A key artefact in this unit was the collaborative discussion, where I was required to critically engage with research questions and interact with peers to apply critical thinking to research methodologies. Another essential component was evaluating different research sources to determine their credibility and reliability. These activities provided a foundation for developing structured academic research skills and ensuring a systematic approach to literature reviews.

Feelings

At the beginning of Unit 2, I felt enthusiastic yet slightly apprehensive about engaging in in-depth academic research. While I had some prior experience with literature reviews, I was not entirely confident in my ability to evaluate sources critically and identify research gaps effectively. The process of engaging with academic texts and distinguishing between credible and non-credible sources initially seemed overwhelming, as it required a detailed understanding of research methodologies and theoretical frameworks.

Participating in the collaborative discussion was both rewarding and somewhat daunting, as I had to clearly articulate my understanding of research principles while also engaging with peers’ viewpoints. However, as the discussion progressed, I found that receiving constructive feedback from my peers enhanced my confidence in analysing academic literature. Their perspectives helped me refine my approach to research evaluation and deepened my understanding of different research methodologies.

Additionally, the ethical considerations in computing research stood out as a crucial aspect of the unit. Understanding that researchers have a responsibility to uphold integrity, transparency, and objectivity reinforced the importance of ethics in academic and professional research. This realisation made me more conscious of how I evaluate sources, cite information, and present research findings unbiasedly.

Evaluation

Overall, Unit 2 was highly beneficial in developing my research and critical evaluation skills. One of my key strengths in this unit was my ability to identify relevant academic sources and assess their credibility effectively. By engaging with peer-reviewed journal articles and research papers, I gained valuable insights into research trends and methodologies within the computing field.

However, one of the challenges I encountered was formulating research questions that were both specific and researchable. Initially, I struggled to narrow down broad research topics into focused and well-defined questions. This was an area where feedback from lecturers and peers was particularly valuable, as it allowed me to refine my research approach.

Another challenge was synthesising information from multiple sources into a cohesive argument. While I successfully identified key themes in literature, I realised that structuring a literature review to connect different studies cohesively required further improvement. This experience highlighted the importance of organising research findings systematically and identifying overarching debates or contradictions in the literature.

Analysis

This unit reinforced the importance of conducting systematic and ethical research in computing. One of the most valuable insights I gained was the role of research questions in guiding academic investigations. Well-formulated research questions are crucial because they determine the scope, direction, and structure of the literature review, research methodology, and data analysis.

The unit also deepened my understanding of source evaluation and credibility assessment. In an era where misinformation is prevalent, the ability to distinguish between credible and unreliable sources is a critical skill. By applying source evaluation frameworks such as the CRAAP test (Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, and Purpose), I learned how to critically analyse research papers, books, and reports to determine their reliability.

Additionally, this unit emphasised the importance of ethical considerations in computing research, particularly concerning data privacy, AI ethics, and responsible research practices. Understanding ethical guidelines from organisations such as the British Computer Society (BCS) and the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) reinforced the importance of integrity, accountability, and transparency in research.

Conclusion

Unit 2 provided a solid foundation in research methodologies, source evaluation, and ethical considerations in computing research. By engaging in collaborative discussions, literature analysis, and research question formulation, I developed a more structured and critical approach to academic investigation.

A key realisation from this unit was that research is not just about collecting information but about critically analysing, synthesising, and interpreting findings. I also recognised the importance of continuously refining research questions and methodologies based on feedback and new insights. While I made progress in evaluating sources and structuring literature reviews, I still need to further develop my ability to synthesise multiple perspectives into well-structured arguments.

Action Plan

To build on the skills and knowledge gained in Unit 2, I will adopt a structured approach to improving my research and critical evaluation skills.

One key area of improvement is enhancing my ability to develop research questions. To achieve this, I will practice formulating clear and researchable questions by engaging in structured brainstorming exercises and seeking feedback from lecturers and peers. I will also use research frameworks such as PICo (Population, Interest, Context) for qualitative research and PICO (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome) for quantitative studies to refine my approach to research question development.

Another area of improvement is literature review synthesis. Instead of merely summarizing sources individually, I will focus on identifying common themes, contradictions, and knowledge gaps across different studies. I will achieve this by using concept mapping and thematic coding techniques to organise and analyse research findings effectively.

Furthermore, I plan to develop stronger critical evaluation skills by engaging in structured critical reading exercises. I will summarise key arguments from academic sources and evaluate their strengths, limitations, and relevance to my research. I will also continue applying the CRAAP test to assess the quality of sources and improve my ability to distinguish between strong and weak evidence.

Finally, I will deepen my ethical awareness in research by exploring additional case studies on ethical dilemmas in computing research, particularly in areas such as AI bias, cybersecurity, and data privacy. I will also stay updated with professional ethical guidelines from BCS and ACM to ensure that my research practices align with industry standards and best practices.

By implementing these strategies, I aim to enhance my research competency, strengthen my literature review skills, and ensure ethical integrity in my academic investigations. These improvements will be particularly beneficial as I progress toward more complex research projects and dissertation writing in later stages of my academic journey.
